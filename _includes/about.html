About

Hi, there! I am Jinpei Guo, a first-year student in the MSML program at Carnegie Mellon University.

I worked closely with Prof. Junchi Yan as a research assistant at SJTU-ReThinkLab. We developed methods to improve the accuracy and efficiency of machine learning models on combinatorial optimization problems, applying language and generative models to graph matching, the traveling salesman problem, maximum independent set, etc.

I also did research with Prof. Xujie Si at the University of Toronto on Neural Logical Reasoning, building reliable and interpretable frameworks to explicitly capture rules of logical puzzles and studying ML for SAT solving with GNNs.

My research interests lie at the intersection of machine learning and combinatorial optimization. I’m also interested in making black-box reasoning and decision-making explainable and reliable by analyzing model structure/inference mathematically, or by coupling ML with exact solvers.
